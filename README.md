Types of UBI:

- Universal Unconditional Basic Income: Regular cash payments provided to all citizens by the state, with no conditions attached. This is the canonical UBI proposal intended to guarantee a minimum standard of living.

- Negative Income Tax (NIT): A system where people earning below a certain threshold receive supplemental payments through the tax system rather than a universal flat payment to everyone.

- Basic Income Guarantee / Citizen's Dividend: Payments funded by public assets or sovereign wealth (for example, revenues from natural resources or a national wealth fund) distributed equally to citizens.

- Conditional Cash Transfers (CCTs): Payments tied to specific behaviors or outcomes (e.g., school attendance, vaccinations). These are not unconditional UBI, but are included because they are a common cash-transfer model.

- Universal Basic Services (UBS): Instead of cash, the state provides a basket of free or subsidized services (healthcare, education, housing, transport) aimed at securing basic needs.

- Job Guarantee (as an alternative/complement): The government ensures employment at a living wage for anyone who wants to work, offering a different route to economic security than unconditional cash.

- Pilot / Time-limited UBI programs: Experimental or regional UBIs run by cities, regions, or nations to test impacts before wider rollout.

- Local / Municipal UBI: UBI programs administered at the city or regional level, often funded by local budgets, taxes, or philanthropic sources.

- Earned Income Supplements (e.g., EITC-style programs): Targeted top-ups to low-income workers rather than universal payments.

- Resource or Sovereign Wealth Dividends: Periodic dividends paid to citizens from resource rents or sovereign wealth funds (e.g., Alaska Permanent Fund model).

- Crypto UBI: Token-based or blockchain-native UBI distributed via airdrops, staking rewards, DAO treasuries, or algorithmic tokens. Crypto UBI can take multiple forms — recurring token distributions to wallet addresses, inflationary token issuance shared among participants, or community-run treasuries that allocate funds based on transparent rules. It can be global, permissionless, and programmable (e.g., conditional disbursements, reputation-weighted payouts), but faces challenges like volatility, access, and regulatory uncertainty.
